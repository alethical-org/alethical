ASKED
What's in the new social media law for kids?
BILL
HF 4138
Signed May 26, 2026
Effective July 1, 2027
Chief authorRep. Peggy Scott →
Companion billSF 4696 →
House 132–2 · Senate 66–0

Minnesota's Stop Harms from Addictive Social Media Act will require parental consent for kids under 16, ban addictive features, and default their accounts to the strictest privacy.

CitedSection 325M.40
1 3(b) — Parental consent
"A covered social media platform may not create an account for a user identified as a child … without first obtaining verifiable parental consent."
2 5(a) — Addictive features
"A covered social media platform may not present addictive interface features in the display or feed of any account of a child."
Such as infinite scrolling, autoplay video, and push notifications
3 4(a) — Privacy by default
"An account for a child shall have all privacy settings set by default at the most private levels."
